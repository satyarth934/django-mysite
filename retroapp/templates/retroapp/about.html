What is the Biomolecular Architect?
The Biomolecular Architect is a retrobiosynthesis tool that not only recommends the reactions (and encoding DNA) to synthesize a molecule, but also recommends molecules that fit a specification (e.g., an octane number of 80 or a binding affinity of 50 nM to a given protein). Recommending molecules that fit a specification is what is called inverse molecular design.
How does it work?
The Biomolecular architect is based in ClusterCAD and Retrotide (in preparation) for retrobiosynthesis and Macaw and Graphdot for molecular inverse design and molecule property prediction. ClusterCAD and Retrotide offer retrobiosynthesis capabilities (i.e. recommendation of reactions and DNA to synthesis a particle) based on polyketide synthases (PKSs). PKSs are modular enzymes that can synthesize a combinatorially large number of molecules (e.g., antibiotics). We will soon complement the PKS retrobiosynthesis capabilities with general biological and chemical retrobiosynthesis so as to cover the largest amount of chemical space. Macaw offers molecular inverse design and property prediction capabilities based on SELFIES sampling and molecular embedding. Graphdot offers molecular property prediction based on graphs.
